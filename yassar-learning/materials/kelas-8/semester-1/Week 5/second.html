New Challenge: Calculating Store Discounts

A store offers a 10% discount on all items. The discount rate is in a single cell (E1). When we drag the formula, something goes wrong! Why?

Price (B)Discount Amount (C)Discount Center (E)
100,000=B2*E1 (Error!)E110%
250,000=B3*E2 (Error!)E2
50,000=B4*E3 (Error!)E3

The reference to E1 keeps shifting to E2, E3, etc., which are empty cells!

The Solution: Absolute Cell References ($)

We need to "lock" the reference to cell E1 so it doesn't change when copied. We use the dollar sign $.

=B2 * $E$1

$E locks **column E**.

$1 locks **row 1**.

The result: $E$1 will always point to cell E1, no matter where the formula is copied!
